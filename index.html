The Core Challenge: The "Cooling Load"

The most important calculation in cold storage design is the "Cooling Load." This determines the size and power of the cooling plant required. It is the total amount of heat from all sources that the system must remove every 24 hours to maintain the set temperature.

The "Shield": Insulation

Insulation's job is to stop heat from leaking IN. Better insulation directly reduces the "Transmission Load," which is the largest continuous drain on energy. A higher R-value means better heat resistance.

Key Insulation Properties

  • Thermal Resistance (R-value): The most important metric. Higher is better.
  • Vapor Barrier: A layer on the *warm* side to stop moisture from getting in and freezing, which destroys R-value.
  • Compressive Strength: Ability to withstand pressure, crucial for floor insulation.
  • Fire Resistance: Critical for safety, as most insulation is foam.

Relative R-Value Comparison

PUR/PIR offers the highest R-value per inch, making it the industry standard for building thin, efficient walls.

One Size Does NOT Fit All

Different products have vastly different storage requirements. Storing bananas at apple temperatures will destroy them. A multi-product facility must have separate, isolated zones for each category.

Key Takeaways

A successful cold storage is a holistic system. The Cooling Load calculation is the foundation, Insulation is the most critical factor for efficiency, and a "One-Size-Fits-All" approach is a recipe for failure. Every design must be tailored to the specific products it protects.
